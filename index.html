David Guetta

Guetta began to DJ in small clubs in France. He played other people's songs, but then discovered house music through the radio in 1987. The next year he started hosting his own club nights. After he became a manager of his own club and organized his own parties. In 2001, Guetta found record label Gum Productions and he released his first single. Guetta's debut album was released in 2002 and it sold over 300,000 copies through Virgin Records. His second album was released in 2004 and his single "Love Don't Let Me Go" was re-released as a mash-up by The Egg in 2006. The mash-up was charted higher than the original single.

In 2007, Guetta's third album Pop Life was released. It sold over 530,000 copies worldwide. The single "Love is Gone" reached number 1 on American Dance Chart and was posted on Billboard Hot 100. In 2009, he placed third in Top 100 DJs by DJ Magazine. Since then Guetta had his own radio show on the internet called RauteMusik. It was soon moved into Radio 538 and aired every Friday evening.(Here is a playlist if you're interested in listening.)

In 2012, Guetta created a new record label known as Jack Back Records, where he intends to focus only on electronic music releases. Forward to 2015, Guetta released his fourth single from his sixth album called "Hey Mama". The single was a hit and reached top ten in the US and UK. Later that year Guetta became the third artist to reach 2 billion streams on Spotify and released a remix of "Hey Mama" to celebrate. In 2016, Guetta released a single called "Would I Lie to You" through Jack Back records and the song uses the vocals/lyrics of 1992 Charles & Eddie hit "Would I Lie to You? ". In 2017, he released another single called "Light My Body Up" featuring rappers Lil' Wayne and Nicki Minaj. Later that year Guetta's next single "2U" was released and featured canadian pop singer Justin Bieber.
